# Competitive Positioning Notes

These notes explain how the website messaging was shaped around visible market references while keeping AID-UP differentiated.

## Market References

- VR medical simulation competitors emphasize realism, scale, and measurable training. SimX describes the "largest library of VR medical simulation cases," and Osso VR publicly highlights surgical training evidence and growth.
- Enterprise records competitors emphasize integrated EHR suites and AI. Oracle Health presents its EHR as integrated with AI-powered clinical intelligence, while large EHR ecosystems compete on comprehensive workflow coverage.
- AI documentation and clinical assistant companies emphasize reducing cognitive load and EHR-integrated outputs. Abridge positions around generative AI for clinical conversations and enterprise deployment.
- Health data platforms are standardizing around FHIR. HL7 describes FHIR as a healthcare data exchange standard, and Microsoft Azure Health Data Services frames its health data platform around FHIR, DICOM, privacy, analytics, and AI.
- MCP is emerging as an open integration pattern for AI tools. Anthropic describes MCP as a protocol for standardizing how applications provide context to language models and connect with data sources and tools.

## AID-UP Differentials Reflected In The Site

1. **Clinical-engineering bundle**
   Large competitors often sell a single platform lane: VR, EHR, documentation AI, or cloud data infrastructure. AID-UP is positioned as a practical partner that can combine clinical insight, software engineering, education, and service delivery.

2. **Pilot-first implementation**
   The site avoids claiming enterprise-scale platform replacement. It instead emphasizes scoped pilots: one simulation, one RAG knowledge base, one records workflow, or one MCP connector.

3. **Interoperability and governance**
   The copy highlights standards-oriented records management, guarded AI access, auditability, and human review so AID-UP does not look like a generic AI automation vendor.

4. **Education plus operations**
   VR simulation is presented as part of a complete training loop: scenario design, VR session, metrics, and curriculum update. This differentiates AID-UP from a page that only says "we build VR."

5. **Physician services as grounding**
   General physician services are not treated as an unrelated offering. The website connects them to clinical workflow understanding, responsible referral, preventive orientation, and product feedback.

6. **Institution-specific integration**
   The site separates hospital, university, and enterprise examples so buyers can recognize their own environment: EHR/FHIR and protocol workflows for hospitals, LMS and OSCE workflows for universities, and MCP/governance workflows for enterprise teams.
